# SEO Off-Page Next Steps

## Backlink Gap Summary

The site is technically solid and commercially focused, but it does not yet show the kind of external authority signals that usually help a specialist delivery site outperform broader competitors. The main missing factor is not more on-page SEO, but more high-quality mentions and links from relevant business, engineering, and product ecosystems.

What the repo can do is make the site more link-worthy:
- clearer positioning for custom web apps, takeover, automation, and senior delivery support
- stronger homepage content
- useful guides, comparisons, templates, and checklists that are worth citing

What the repo cannot do:
- create real backlinks
- create brand mentions
- generate third-party trust
- replace earned authority with schema or keyword repetition

## 10 Realistic Link Acquisition Ideas

1. Publish a short article on takeover of inherited business software and pitch it to Czech product, startup, or engineering newsletters.
2. Turn the takeover checklist into a promoted asset and offer it to founders, CTOs, and product leads as a practical reference.
3. Write a guest post comparing internal tool development vs. SaaS for a relevant B2B or product publication.
4. Publish a technical-commercial teardown of how to scope a first release for business-critical software and share it on LinkedIn.
5. Reach out to agencies that do branding or marketing but not custom app delivery and propose partner referrals with a link from their partner page.
6. Offer a concise expert quote or commentary for podcasts, newsletters, or founder communities discussing failed software projects or takeover risk.
7. Build one anonymised but highly practical case-study article around a takeover or automation pattern and promote it as a reference asset.
8. Submit high-quality answers and resources to curated directories or local Czech business ecosystems where independent senior specialists are listed.
9. Create a useful comparison asset around contract developer vs. agency and pitch it to buyers evaluating software delivery models.
10. Turn one checklist or guide into a downloadable PDF and use it in outreach to operations leaders, product teams, and technical decision-makers.
